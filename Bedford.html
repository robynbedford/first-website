Personal Life

Early Childhood

My name is Robyn Bedford, I was born on March 17, 1992 in a small city east of Toronto called Cornwall, Ontario. Childhood was very busy as I was raised in a family of six, however I learned a lot from my older siblings. Early on in life I had always dreamed of being a Marine Biologist, however after highschool life took me on a different path.

Adolescence

Following highschool I decided to set my dream aside of being a Marine Biologist to study Nursing at Trent University. After completing my Bachelors of Science in Nursing I discovered it was not the ideal career path for me, so I decided to move abroad and teach English in South Korea.

Early Adulthood

Working abroad gave me the opportunity to explore different parts of the continent and helped broaden my horizons. It also introduced me to many individuals with a wide range of careers. Upon returning from South Korea, a job opportunity became available in Yellowknife, NT to work as an Education Assistant. After being convinced by a friend to move up North, I applied for this job and made the move. While working up North I attempted to create my own Website as a means of displaying content from travel. However, with lack of knowledge and skill I got discouraged.

Six months after my move to Yellowknife I lost a sibling and moved back home to Ontario. This is where my interest in web development grew, as I worked a temporary job for the City of Cornwall Tourism Department. Management at this job had me updating certain web pages on their website. Shortly after starting this job I began looking into Web Development programs and was introduced to the one at Humber College by a student who attended in 2017. Although she said the program was challenging, she recommended I apply as it was worth the investment. Fast forward nine months and I am thrilled to begin my new journey in Web Design and Development.
